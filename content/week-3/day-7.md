## Revelation

Easter has now passed — but the commission it carried has not. The risen Christ did not appear to His disciples and invite them to a quiet, private celebration. He appeared, showed them His wounds, breathed on them, and sent them. The resurrection is the most explosive event in human history, and explosive events do not remain contained. The stone was rolled away not just to let Jesus out — but to let the witnesses in, so they could run and tell. That mandate has not expired.

Matthew 28:18 contains the statement that grounds everything: all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. The commission is not built on human enthusiasm or institutional strategy. It is built on the total, complete, universal authority of the One who defeated death. Every power, every system, every principality — all of it is under His feet. And from that position of absolute authority, He says: therefore go. The commission flowed from the resurrection. You go because He reigns — and He reigns today, on the other side of Easter, just as He did on Sunday morning.

Luke 24:48 says simply: you are witnesses of these things. A witness is not someone who has a theory. A witness is someone who has seen. You have reflected this week on the cross, the tomb, the silence, the descent, the resurrection, the new life. Something has been deposited in you. And Acts 1:8 gives the promise that powers the going: you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you. Not by your own strength. Clothed in power from on high. Go. He is risen — and the world still needs to hear it.

## Morning Prayer

Risen Lord, I stand on the other side of Easter — having reflected this week on the cross, the empty tomb, the silence of Holy Saturday, the depth of Your descent, and the blazing reality of Your resurrection. I am not the same. You have done something in me through these reflections that I receive with open hands.

You are risen. And because You are risen, all authority is Yours — not just on Easter Sunday, but today, and every ordinary day that follows. Every fear I carry about the future, every uncertainty about my calling, every inadequacy I feel for the mission You have placed in me — I lay it at the feet of the One who holds all authority in heaven and on earth.

Send me. Into my neighbourhood, my workplace, my research, my community, my relationships. Make me a witness — not just in my words but in the quality of my life, the depth of my joy, the reality of my peace. Let people look at me and see someone who has been with the risen Christ. I carry Easter Monday forward.

## Daily Practices

- Read Luke 24:13–35 — the road to Emmaus. Walk with the disciples from confusion to recognition to commission. Note the moment they recognised Him and what they did immediately afterwards.
- Review your journal from the whole week. Write one sentence that captures the deepest thing God said to you. This is your testimony from this week. Keep it.
- Share the resurrection with someone today — in words, in kindness, in an act of generosity that can only be explained by Easter. Let your life be the evidence.
- Commit to carry one truth from this week into the month ahead. Write it somewhere you will see it daily. The resurrection is not an annual event. It is a permanent reality you inhabit.
