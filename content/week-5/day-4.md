## Revelation

Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God. This promise is breathtaking — and impossibly demanding if we have to meet it ourselves. Who can claim purity of heart? Who has a heart with no hidden motives, no double vision, no divided loyalty? The answer is: no one — except by the blood of Jesus. Hebrews 10 makes the remarkable claim that our hearts can be sprinkled to cleanse us from a guilty conscience. The blood of Christ reaches the interior — not just the external behaviour but the heart itself, making possible what no human effort could achieve: genuine, interior purity.

And the promise attached to purity of heart is the most intimate of all the Beatitudes: they will see God. Not see about God. Not hear about God. See Him. This is the telos — the ultimate destination — of the entire Christian life. Every act of confession, every act of repentance, every choice of holiness, every moment of trust in difficulty — all of it is oriented toward this: a heart so purified by the blood that it can see clearly, without obstruction, the face of the One it was made for.

The seventh Beatitude — peacemakers — flows naturally from having received the ministry of reconciliation. You were made peace with God through the cross. That reconciliation was given to you. And now it becomes your ministry. 2 Corinthians 5 makes the staggering claim that God makes His appeal through you — that when you work to restore broken relationships, to bring peace where there is conflict, to reconcile what has been divided, you are carrying out the very work that Christ accomplished at Golgotha. You are not just doing good. You are being what you were made to be: a child of God.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I want to see You. Not just know about You — see You. And I understand today that purity of heart is the precondition for that vision. So I come to the blood of Jesus and ask that what no willpower of mine could achieve, the cross might accomplish: a heart made clean, a conscience made clear, an interior life free from the double vision of divided loyalties.

Sprinkle my heart clean today. Not by my discipline alone, but by the power of what was poured out at Calvary. Let there be nothing in me that prevents me from seeing You clearly. Remove the cataracts of unconfessed sin, the fog of distraction, the cloudiness of a heart that has been looking in too many directions at once. I want to see You.

And make me a peacemaker. Not a conflict-avoider — a peacemaker. Someone who actively, intentionally carries the reconciling work of the cross into the broken relationships and divided communities around me. Let Your appeal come through me. Let the world see, in the way I make peace, what it looks like to be a child of God.

## Daily Practices

- Read Psalm 24:3–4 — "Who may ascend the mountain of the Lord? The one who has clean hands and a pure heart." Then read Hebrews 10:19–22 as God's own answer to that requirement. Let the two passages speak to each other.
- Identify one relationship in your life that is currently marked by conflict, distance, or unresolved tension. Pray specifically about it today. Ask God how He is calling you to be a peacemaker — not just a peace-keeper — in that situation.
- Journal: "What are the divided loyalties in my heart right now — the places where I am trying to see God and something else at the same time?" Bring each one honestly to the blood of Jesus.
- Read 2 Corinthians 5:14–21 in full — Paul's complete theology of reconciliation. Let "we are therefore Christ's ambassadors" become not an abstract idea but a present-tense identity you carry into your day.
