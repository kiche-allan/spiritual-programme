## Revelation

Trust is easy when everything is going well. The test — and the invitation — comes when the path you are on makes no sense, when the circumstances feel like contradiction, when God seems silent precisely when you need Him to be loudest. Proverbs 3:5–6 does not say trust God when you understand what He is doing. It says trust Him with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding. This is a direct, conscious act of surrender — not blind naivety, but anchored confidence in a God whose vision is higher than yours.

Romans 8:28 is perhaps the most quietly radical verse in the New Testament. Notice what it does not say — it does not say all things are good. Some circumstances are genuinely painful, genuinely unjust, genuinely hard. But it says God works all things together for good. The word works is the Greek synergei — He is actively, continuously, sovereignly weaving everything — including the things that have broken your heart — into something redemptive. You cannot always see the weaving from the inside of the tapestry. But He sees it from above, and He has not stopped working.

Philippians 4:6–7 gives the daily practice of trust: bring everything to God in prayer, with thanksgiving even in the difficulty, and receive in return a peace that transcends understanding — a peace that does not require you to understand the situation in order to experience it. This is the gift of trusting God in hard places: not that the storm immediately stops, but that you are guarded in the middle of it.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I bring You today the things I do not understand. The prayers that have not yet been answered the way I hoped. The circumstances that still feel unresolved. The silence that has sometimes felt like absence. I lay all of it before You — not with resignation, but with trust.

I choose, today, to lean not on my own understanding. My understanding is limited. My vision is partial. But You see the end from the beginning, and You are working in the in-between. I believe — help my unbelief — that You are weaving even this into something good.

Guard my heart with Your peace today. Not the peace that comes from resolution, but the peace that comes from knowing that You hold all things. I will not be anxious. I present my requests to You with thanksgiving — even now — because You are worthy of thanks in every season.

## Daily Practices

- Read Romans 8:18–39 in full. Let Paul's argument build — suffering, Spirit, hope, and the unshakeable love of God. Note where the passage lands: nothing can separate us from the love of God.
- Name the most difficult circumstance you are currently navigating. Write it down. Then write Romans 8:28 directly beneath it. God is working in this too.
- Practise Philippians 4:6 literally today: present one specific worry to God with a specific act of thanksgiving alongside it. Notice what shifts in your interior when you add the thanksgiving.
- Read the story of Joseph in Genesis 50:15–21. His summary of his life — "you intended to harm me, but God intended it for good" — is what Romans 8:28 looks like lived out. Let it build your faith for what you are currently in the middle of.
