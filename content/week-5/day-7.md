## Revelation

Immediately after the Beatitudes, Jesus speaks two declarations that are easy to miss in their grammatical structure. He does not say: try to be salt. He does not say: you should become the light of the world. He says: you are the salt of the earth. You are the light of the world. These are statements of identity, not instructions for behaviour. The Beatitudes describe what you are becoming through the work of the cross; salt and light describe what you therefore are in the world — not what you must strive to achieve, but what you already are when the cross has done its work.

Revelation 1:5–6 ties the blood and the identity together with breathtaking clarity. By His blood, you have been freed from your sins. And by that same act, you have been made something: a kingdom, a priest, someone who serves the living God. The blood is not only redemptive — it is constitutive. It does not merely remove what was wrong. It establishes what you now are. You have been made to be a kingdom. You carry within you the reign of God everywhere you go.

The closing word of the Beatitudes section is commission: let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven. Notice the ultimate purpose — not that they will glorify you, not even that they will glorify your good works, but that they will glorify your Father in heaven. The whole architecture of the Beatitudes, the cross, the blood, the purpose — it is all oriented toward this: a world that looks at lives transformed by the grace of God and is moved to worship the One who made that transformation possible. This is the purpose you were made for. Go and live it.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I close this week not with a sense of arrival but with a sense of sending. I have sat with the Beatitudes — and I have seen in them a portrait of Jesus, and a portrait of who I am being made into by the power of the cross. I have stood at the blood and received what it purchased: freedom, cleansing, a new identity, an open way into Your presence.

I go into this coming week as salt — not trying to be salty, but carrying in myself the preserving, flavour-giving character of a life shaped by the kingdom. I go as light — not performing visibility, but shining with the natural luminosity of someone in whom Christ lives. The Beatitudes are not goals I am pursuing. They are the shape that Your grace is pressing into me, day by day, through every difficult circumstance and every quiet act of obedience.

Let my life this week point people to You. Let them see something in me — in my meekness, my mercy, my purity of intention, my willingness to make peace — that they cannot explain apart from God. And let the only explanation they can find be the right one: that I have been with Jesus, covered by His blood, and shaped by His character. To Him be glory and power for ever and ever.

## Daily Practices

- Review the whole week — which Beatitude landed most deeply for you? Which one feels most like the area where God is currently doing His most significant work? Write it down and carry it into the month ahead.
- The blood of Jesus appeared throughout this week — cleansing, speaking, overcoming, making you a kingdom and priest. Write one sentence that captures what the blood means to you personally, in your own words. Return to it whenever accusation rises.
- Share one insight from this week with someone close to you. The word of your testimony is a weapon. Use it deliberately and generously.
- Read Matthew 5:1–16 in one sitting as a closing meditation — from the Beatitudes to salt and light. Let it be a mirror. Let it be a map. Let it send you.
