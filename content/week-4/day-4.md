## Revelation

Yesterday we established the theology of holiness — what it is and why it matters. Today we bring it down to the ground, into the commute, into the conversation, into the screen time, into the small unobserved choices that no one sees but that are the actual substance of a holy life. Colossians 3:17 is one of the most radical verses in the New Testament in its scope: whatever you do — in word or deed — do it in the name of the Lord Jesus. Not just your church activities. Not just your quiet times. Everything.

Ephesians 4:22–24 describes holiness as a daily practice of putting off and putting on. The old self — its habits, its reflexes, its patterns — is something you actively, deliberately take off, like a garment. The new self — created to be like God in true righteousness and holiness — is something you actively, deliberately put on. This is not passive waiting for holiness to descend on you. It is daily, intentional cooperation with the work of the Holy Spirit.

The word "sanctified" in 1 Thessalonians 4:3 is the same root as holy. God's will for you is your sanctification. Not your success, not your comfort, not your reputation — your becoming more like Him. This reframes every difficulty you face this week. The hard conversation, the temptation resisted, the kindness shown when unkindness would have been easier — these are not interruptions to your spiritual life. They are your spiritual life.

## Morning Prayer

Lord, I take off the old self today. Deliberately and intentionally — the impatience, the pride, the careless speech, the habitual compromises I barely notice anymore. I take them off and I lay them down. They are not who I am. They are the residue of who I used to be.

And I put on the new self — created to be like You in true righteousness and holiness. I put on gentleness for my interactions today. Integrity for my work. Patience for the frustrations I will encounter. Purity for what I allow through my eyes and my mind.

Let everything I do today — every email, every conversation, every decision, every moment of leisure — be done in Your name, as an act of worship. Make my ordinary day holy.

## Daily Practices

- Audit one area of ordinary life — your phone use, your entertainment choices, your speech patterns — through the lens of Colossians 3:17. What would it look like to do that thing in the name of the Lord Jesus?
- Practise the put off / put on discipline from Ephesians 4:22–24 in real time today. When you catch yourself in an old pattern, name it, put it off, and consciously choose the new self response instead.
- Read Colossians 3:5–17 in full — Paul's complete list of what to take off and what to put on. Use it as a practical inventory for your day.
- Journal at the end of the day: "Where did I live in the old self today? Where did I live in the new self? What made the difference between the two?"
