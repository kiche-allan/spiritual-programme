## Revelation

Having spent five days sitting with the Beatitudes and the blood of Jesus, we arrive at Saturday with a question that the whole week has been building toward: what is all of this for? The answer is both simpler and more expansive than we often think. The purpose of the Christian life is not primarily what you do — it is who you become. And Romans 8:29 names that becoming with extraordinary precision: conformed to the image of His Son.

The Beatitudes are not a programme for spiritual improvement. They are a portrait of a person who looks like Jesus. Poor in spirit — like Christ who emptied Himself. Mourning over sin — like Christ who wept over Jerusalem. Meek — like Christ who submitted to the cross. Hungry for righteousness — like Christ whose food was to do the Father's will. Merciful — like Christ who forgave from the cross. Pure in heart — like Christ who had no sin. A peacemaker — like Christ who is our peace. Persecuted for righteousness — like Christ who was crucified for it. The Beatitudes are the character of Jesus, reproduced in those who have been transformed by His cross and His blood.

Ephesians 2:10 says the works were prepared in advance — and they were prepared for a person of this character. You do not find your purpose and then develop the character to fulfil it. You develop the character, and the purpose becomes clear. Jeremiah 29:11 is a promise of plans — but it is a promise made to people in exile, in difficulty, in the middle of circumstances that made the future look impossible. The purpose of God is not deterred by your current circumstances. It was settled before time, secured by the cross, and is being worked out in the very process of your being conformed to the image of Christ.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I receive today the clarity that this week has been building toward. My purpose is not primarily a role to play or a platform to build or a career to construct. My purpose is to be conformed to the image of Your Son — and from that conformity, to walk in the specific good works You have prepared for me before the foundation of the world.

I look at the Beatitudes this morning and I see Jesus. Poor in spirit, meek, merciful, pure, a peacemaker, willing to be persecuted for righteousness. This is who the cross is making me. Slowly, sometimes painfully, always faithfully — You are pressing the image of Your Son into the clay of my life. Do not stop. Even where it hurts. Especially where it hurts.

And I trust the plans You have for me. Not because they are always comfortable or always clear, but because they come from You — and You have proven Your faithfulness at the cross. The God who did not spare His own Son in order to secure my purpose will not now abandon that purpose when the circumstances look uncertain.

## Daily Practices

- Read Romans 8:28–30 slowly and carefully. Notice the golden chain: foreknew → predestined → called → justified → glorified. Where are you in that chain today? How does knowing where it ends change how you face the present?
- Look back at the Beatitudes as a whole — Matthew 5:3–12. Place your name mentally in each one. Which qualities are already visible in you? Which are still being formed? Journal honestly about both.
- Identify one good work — from Ephesians 2:10 — that you sense God has specifically prepared for you in this season. Name it as concretely as you can. Then ask: what character quality from the Beatitudes does this work require of me?
- Read Colossians 1:27 — "Christ in you, the hope of glory." Let this be your purpose statement for the week ahead. Not what you do, but Who is in you and what He is becoming through you.
