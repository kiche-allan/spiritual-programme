## Revelation

The Apostles' Creed contains a phrase that is often glossed over: He descended into hell. Whatever the precise theological meaning of that descent, the spiritual truth it carries is enormous: Christ went all the way down. He did not hover above the darkness and offer instructions from a safe distance. He entered it. He descended into the lowest place so that no place you can fall would be beyond His reach.

Hebrews 2:14–15 reveals the strategy of the incarnation and the cross. To destroy death, Christ shared in humanity — in flesh, in blood, in mortality. He entered the domain of the enemy and defeated him from the inside. Death could not hold Him because He had no sin — and yet He entered death fully, completely, bearing all our sin, so that when He emerged, He brought the keys with Him. The fear of death — that great enslaver of human souls — has been broken. Not eventually. Now.

Psalm 139:8 holds the implication of all this for your daily life. If you make your bed in the depths — in depression, in grief, in shame, in failure, in the lowest place you have ever been — He is there. There is no bottom to human suffering that Christ has not already touched and transformed by His presence. You are never in a place where He cannot find you. You are never in a darkness He has not already entered. And from every depth, He has already begun the ascent.

## Morning Prayer

Lord Jesus, the depth of Your love astonishes me. You did not send rescue from a safe distance — You descended. You went all the way into the lowest place so that wherever I fall, You are already there, waiting, with Your hand outstretched.

I bring to You today the deepest places in me — the depths I do not show anyone else. The grief that has not lifted. The shame I still carry in secret. The fear that still grips me in the night. I bring every depth, because I know that in every depth, You are already present.

You broke the power of death itself. The fear of death — of endings, of loss, of the unknown — no longer has the final word in my life. You hold the keys. And because You ascended from the depths, You are taking me with You. Upward. Always upward.

## Amen

In the name of the One who descended and ascended, Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read Hebrews 2:9–18 in full — Christ made like His brothers in every way, so He could be a merciful High Priest who is able to help those who are being tempted.
- Identify one depth in your life — a place of grief, shame, or fear. Write it down. Then write Psalm 139:8 alongside it. He is there too.
- Meditate on Revelation 1:18 — "I hold the keys of death and Hades." The One who descended now holds authority over the very place He entered. Let that authority speak to whatever feels most locked in your life.
- Journal: "What does it mean for my daily life that Christ has been in my lowest places — and did not stay there? What does His ascent say about mine?"
