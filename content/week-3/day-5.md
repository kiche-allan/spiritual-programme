## Revelation

Paul does not soften the stakes. If Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile, you are still in your sins, and those who have died in Christ have simply perished. The resurrection is not a bonus feature of Christianity — it is the load-bearing wall. Remove it and the entire structure collapses. But Christ has indeed been raised. That single fact — historically attested, personally experienced by hundreds of eyewitnesses — has changed the architecture of all of reality.

The word "firstfruits" in 1 Corinthians 15:20 is agricultural and legal. The firstfruits were the first portion of the harvest, offered to God as a pledge that the rest was coming. Christ's resurrection was not an isolated miracle — it was the first instalment of a total harvest. His rising guarantees your rising. His new body is the prototype of yours. Death has not been merely paused — it has been defeated as a principle, permanently, with consequences that will unfold across all of eternity.

Romans 6:4 brings the resurrection off the future calendar and into this morning. You were buried with Christ in baptism — and you were raised with Him. The new life is not something you are waiting to receive. It is something you are already living, if you know it. The invitation of the resurrection today is to live from the reality of your rising rather than the memory of your dying. You are not a sinner trying to become a saint. You are a new creation learning to walk in what you already are.

## Morning Prayer

Risen Lord, I come to You today not at a cross, not at a sealed tomb, but at an empty one. The grave could not hold You. Death could not contain You. And because You are risen, everything has changed — including me.

Where, O death, is your victory? I taunt it today, not in arrogance but in the authority that You have given me. The sting of sin has been extracted. The power of the law to condemn has been broken. I stand not in my own righteousness but in Yours — resurrection righteousness, undefeatable and eternal.

Let me live today from the reality of my rising. Let me not crawl through this day as one still bound in grave clothes. I have been raised. Let my choices, my confidence, my generosity, my joy — let all of it reflect the life of One who has conquered death.

## Amen

He is risen. He is risen indeed. Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read 1 Corinthians 15 in full — Paul's entire resurrection chapter. Let the argument build and land completely. Do not skim.
- Read John 20:1–18 — the empty tomb and Mary's encounter with the risen Christ. Sit with her moment of recognition when He says her name.
- Journal: "If I truly believed I was already raised with Christ, what would I do differently today? What grave clothes am I still wearing that the resurrection says I can take off?"
- Speak 1 Corinthians 15:55–57 aloud as a declaration over every area of your life where death has had a foothold. Name each area and speak the victory over it specifically.
