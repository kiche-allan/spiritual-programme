## Revelation

Colossians 3 begins with the word "since" — and that word carries enormous weight. Not "if you have been raised." Not "when you eventually get there." Since. It is an accomplished fact, a completed reality, a settled matter. You have already been raised with Christ. The question Paul is pressing is not whether you have resurrection life — it is whether you are living from it. Are your heart and mind oriented toward the reality of where you now are, or toward the reality of what you used to be?

Galatians 2:20 is perhaps the most radical statement of Christian identity in all of Scripture. I no longer live. The person who existed before Christ — defined by sin, by self, by the old patterns — that person died at the cross. What lives now is Christ living in and through the vessel of your body. This is not metaphor. This is not aspiration. This is the literal spiritual anatomy of a person in Christ. You are not trying to get Christ into you — He is already there. You are not working to become holy — you are learning to manifest what is already true.

Romans 8:11 closes this movement with the most personal promise of all. The very Spirit who raised Christ from physical death is living inside you. The power of the resurrection is not something that happened two thousand years ago and is available to you in theory — it is a present, active, indwelling force. The same energy that rolled the stone away is the energy in which you walk today. Live accordingly.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I rest in this truth today: I have been raised. I am not trying to be raised. I am not hoping to be raised one day. Since — since — I have been raised with Christ, my life is hidden with Him in God. I am held in the most secure place in the universe.

Let me live from this today. Not from the memory of my failures. Not from the fear of what might go wrong. Not from the exhausted place of someone still trying to earn what has already been given. From resurrection. From freedom. From the fullness of Christ who lives in me.

And let the same Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead quicken my mortal body today — my tired places, my weak places, my discouraged places. Breathe resurrection life into everything that feels dead. I am not a tomb. I am a temple. Inhabit me fully.

## Amen

In the name of the risen Christ who lives in me, Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read Colossians 3:1–17 in full — what resurrection life looks like put on, day by day, in practical terms. Note what to put off and what to put on.
- Write out Galatians 2:20 in your own words as a personal statement of identity. Read it aloud over yourself. Let it be more true to you than your feelings today.
- Identify one area of your life you have been living from death rather than resurrection — old patterns, old fears, old identity. Bring it to God and ask Him to breathe life into it.
- Do something today that is an expression of resurrection joy — generous, creative, alive. Let every day carry Easter forward. The resurrection is not a season. It is a permanent reality.
