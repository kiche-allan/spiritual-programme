## Revelation

Holy Saturday is the most overlooked day in the Christian calendar. The cross was behind the disciples. The resurrection had not yet come. They did not know what we now know — that Sunday was coming. They sat in the wreckage of their hopes, in the silence of a sealed tomb, wondering if everything they believed was wrong. This is not a theological abstraction. This is the experience of every believer who has prayed and not yet received an answer, who has stood at a grave and not yet seen resurrection.

Lamentations 3 was written in the ruins of Jerusalem — the city destroyed, the temple gone, the people in exile. And yet from inside that devastation, the writer found this: His compassions never fail. They are new every morning. This is not naive optimism. It is the hardest, most costly kind of faith — faith that holds onto the character of God when the circumstances contradict it. The Lord is my portion. Therefore I will wait. Not because waiting is easy. Because He is trustworthy.

Psalm 22 — the very psalm Jesus quoted from the cross — does not end in desolation. It turns. By verse 24, the Psalmist has arrived at the truth that God has not hidden His face, that He has listened. Every Holy Saturday in your life — every season of silence, every sealed tomb — carries within it the structure of Psalm 22: a cry in the dark, and then a turn. Sunday always comes. It came for Christ. It will come for you too.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I reflect today on the Saturday spaces of my own life — the places where I have cried out and not yet seen the answer, where the tomb still feels sealed, where Sunday seems far away. I do not pretend this is easy. I bring You the honest weight of waiting.

But I say with the writer of Lamentations, from inside the ruins: Your compassions have not failed. Your mercies are new this morning — even this morning. You are my portion. And because You are my portion, I will wait for You. Not because I have no other options, but because You are worth waiting for.

Remind me today that You have not hidden Your face from me. That You have heard every cry I have prayed in the dark. That what feels like silence is not absence. Sunday came for Christ — and it will come for every area of my life where the tomb is still sealed.

## Daily Practices

- Read Psalm 22 in full — from the cry of dereliction to the turn of praise. Do not skip to the praise. Let the descent be real before the turn arrives.
- Name one area of your life that is currently in a Holy Saturday — between a word from God and its fulfilment. Hold it before Him today without forcing a resolution.
- Read Lamentations 3:1–33. Let the descent into grief and the turn toward hope both be real to you. Note where the turn happens and what triggers it.
- Journal: "What does faithfulness to God look like when I cannot yet see what He is doing? What would it mean to call Him my portion today, in this season?"
