## Revelation

Ecclesiastes is the book of a man who searched everywhere for purpose and came back with dust in his hands — until the very last verse. After chapters exploring pleasure, wealth, wisdom, work, and legacy, Solomon lands here: fear God and keep His commandments. This is everything. Not as a religious formality — as the distilled conclusion of a lifetime of searching.

Micah 6:8 gives this same truth three dimensions: act justly, love mercy, walk humbly. These three are not three separate callings. They are one integrated posture — a life that is correctly oriented toward God through humility, and therefore correctly oriented toward people through justice and mercy. Purpose is not primarily about your career or your platform. It is about the quality of your being in every interaction, every day.

Colossians 3:23 is the daily application: whatever you do — your research, your work, your relationships, your small invisible acts — do it with your whole heart, as if God Himself is watching, because He is. The fear of the Lord transforms your motivation. You are no longer performing for an audience of people. You are working for an audience of One. And that shift in audience changes everything — the quality of your effort, the integrity of your choices, the patience of your process.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I have sometimes looked for my purpose in grand visions and dramatic moments. But Your Word brings me back to something simpler and deeper: fear You, walk humbly, love mercy, do justice. Let this be the shape of my days.

Today I consecrate my ordinary. Every email, every conversation, every task — I offer it to You as an act of worship. Let the fear of You purify my motivations. Let me not work for applause or approval, but for the quiet satisfaction of knowing that You are pleased.

And where my purposes have been small and self-serving, expand them. Let me see that the good works You prepared for me include the way I treat the people around me today — the small, uncelebrated acts of justice and mercy that no one will notice but You.

## Daily Practices

- Read Ecclesiastes 1:1–2 and 12:9–14. Let the journey from vanity to conclusion speak to you. Solomon tested everything before he landed where he landed. What has your own searching taught you?
- Reflect honestly: in your work this week, were you performing for people or working for God? Where did the two audiences pull you in different directions?
- Identify one act of justice or mercy you can do today — specific, practical, small. Do it without telling anyone. Let it be entirely between you and God.
- Journal: "What would my ordinary days look like if I truly believed God was my only audience? What would change in how I work, speak, and relate to people?"
