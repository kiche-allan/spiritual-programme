## Revelation

We move now from the fear of God into the intimacy of God — and the transition is not a contradiction. It is the same coin, both sides. The God before whom we bow in holy awe is the same God who says to you: I knew you before I formed you. You were not an afterthought. You were not an accident. Before your parents had a conversation, before the cells that made you were arranged, God already knew your name.

Psalm 139 is perhaps the most intimate passage in all of Scripture. God knows when you sit down and when you stand up. He perceives your thoughts before they fully form. He knit you together in your mother's womb with specific intentionality. And Isaiah 49:16 contains one of the most astonishing images in the Bible — your name, your very self, engraved on the palms of God's hands. Not written in a book somewhere. Not stored in a file. Engraved. Permanent. On His hands — the very hands that hold all things together.

This knowing is the foundation of your purpose. You cannot walk confidently into what God has called you to do if you are not first settled in the truth that He knows you completely and loves you fully. Purpose does not begin with a mission statement. It begins with being known. And you are known — more thoroughly, more tenderly, more permanently than any human relationship has ever known you.

## Morning Prayer

Father, the thought that You knew me before I existed — that my name was on Your heart before it was on my birth certificate — undoes me in the best possible way. I am not searching for an identity. You have already given me one.

Where I have felt unknown, unseen, or overlooked by the world, remind me today that You see everything. You know my sit-downs and my stand-ups. You know the thought I have not yet spoken. And still — still — You engrave my name on Your palms.

Let me rest today in being known. Not striving to be understood, not performing to be seen — just resting, deeply and quietly, in the knowledge that the God of all creation knows me by name.

## Daily Practices

- Read Psalm 139 in full, slowly. Pause at every verse that resonates. Do not rush. Let the intimacy of each line land before you move to the next.
- Journal: "What does it mean for my daily life that God knew me before I was born? Does this truth change how I see my failures? My gifts? My struggles?"
- Identify one area where you have been seeking validation from people rather than resting in God's knowing. Pray over it specifically. Ask God to make His knowing more real to you than the opinions of others.
- Speak Isaiah 49:16 aloud — insert your own name into it. "See, I have engraved [your name] on the palms of my hands." Let the weight of that settle for a full minute before moving on.
