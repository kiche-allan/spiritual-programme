## Revelation

The command to be holy is the most repeated moral command in all of Scripture. And yet it is perhaps the most misunderstood. For many believers, holiness conjures images of religious restriction — a long list of things you cannot do. But that is not the biblical portrait of holiness. Holiness is not primarily about what you avoid. It is about what you resemble.

The root of the Hebrew word for holy — qadosh — means set apart, distinct, other. God is holy not because He follows a set of rules but because He is fundamentally different from everything that is broken, corrupted, or fallen. When He calls you to holiness, He is calling you to resemble Him — to carry something of that otherness into the world. You were not designed to blend in. You were designed to be distinctly, visibly different in a way that points to your Father.

Romans 12:1–2 reveals the pathway and the motive. The motive is not fear or obligation — it is mercy. In view of God's mercy, offer yourself. The cross has already been established; holiness is your response to it, not your attempt to earn it. And the pathway is the renewing of your mind — transformation from the inside out. A holy life is not manufactured by willpower. It is grown by dwelling so deeply in God's presence that His nature increasingly becomes yours.

## Morning Prayer

Holy Father, Your very name is holiness. And You have called me — not reluctantly, not as a long shot — but intentionally and confidently, to be holy as You are holy. I receive that call today not as a burden but as an invitation into the deepest version of who I was made to be.

I offer myself to You this morning as a living sacrifice. Not a dead ritual, not a performance — a living, breathing act of worship in which my body, my mind, my habits, my desires, my relationships, and my time are placed in Your hands. Do with them what only You can do.

Renew my mind. Where the patterns of the world have shaped my thinking more than Your Word has, disrupt them. Let the holiness that is already in You — living in me by Your Spirit — press outward and become visible in how I live.

## Daily Practices

- Read Leviticus 19 — holiness made shockingly practical. Note how many of the commands involve how you treat other people. Holiness is not private; it is relational.
- Identify one area of your life where you have been conforming to the pattern of this world rather than being transformed by the renewing of your mind. Bring it specifically to God today.
- Journal: "What would a holy version of my ordinary week look like? What would change in my habits, my speech, my entertainment, my relationships?"
- Read 1 Thessalonians 4:3–7 and note that God's will for you is your sanctification. It is not ambiguous. Receive the clarity of that today — He has already decided what He wants for you.
