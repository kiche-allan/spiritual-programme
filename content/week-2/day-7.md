## Revelation

On the Lord's Day, all the threads of this week are woven into one. You have sat with the fear of God — understanding what it is and what it produces. You have wrestled with temptation and stood on the declaration that sin is not your master. You have trusted God in the difficult circumstances that still surround you. You have received the truth that you are known before birth and called by name. You have seen that your purpose is already prepared and irrevocable. You have been invited into justice, mercy, and humility as the daily shape of that purpose.

Now Jesus speaks the commission: as the Father has sent me, I am sending you. This is not a vague spiritual concept. It is a direct, personal, present-tense sending. And 2 Corinthians 5:11 shows us something remarkable: it is the fear of the Lord that fuels the mission. Paul says, because we know what it is to fear the Lord, we try to persuade others. The awe of God is not a private, interior experience that keeps you sequestered in quiet reflection. It is the very thing that propels you outward into the world.

And Isaiah 8:13–14 closes the week with the most tender promise of all: the God you fear will be your sanctuary. The One before whom you bow is the One in whom you are completely safe. You are sent — but you are never sent alone. The fear of God is not a burden. It is a shelter. Go this week carrying both: the holy weight of His greatness, and the warm security of His nearness.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I close this week undone — in the very best way. You have shown me Your greatness and Your nearness. You have reminded me that I was known before I was formed, called before I could respond, and equipped before I even understood the assignment.

I go into this coming week as someone who has sat with You. I carry the fear of You not as a weight but as a compass — it will keep me oriented when the noise of life tries to pull me off course. It will purify my motivations when I am tempted to work for lesser audiences. It will be my sanctuary when the pressures of purpose feel too great.

Send me, Lord. Into my work, my relationships, my community, my calling — send me as You sent the Son: with full knowledge of the Father's heart, sustained by the Father's presence, anchored in the Father's purpose. Let my life this week be a poem that reflects its Author.

## Amen

In Jesus' name — with holy fear and joyful purpose — Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Review your journal from all seven days. What single sentence captures what God said to you this week? Write it somewhere you will return to.
- Who in your life needs to hear something from these themes? Share one insight with them today — a verse, a prayer, a word of what God has been doing in you.
- Name one concrete way the fear of God will change how you work or relate to people in the week ahead. Be specific — not "I will be more holy" but a named action in a named context.
- Commit one verse from this week to memory. Let it be the verse that cut deepest — the one that is still in you. Carry it into Monday.
