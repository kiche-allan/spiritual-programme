## Revelation

There is a lie that many believers carry silently: that their struggle with temptation is evidence that they are not truly saved, not truly spiritual, not truly close to God. Scripture dismantles this entirely. 1 Corinthians 10:13 does not say that the mature Christian is never tempted. It says that every temptation comes with a way out, and that God is faithful to provide it. The struggle itself is not the problem. What you do with it is.

Romans 6:14 is a declaration of your legal standing before God. Sin is no longer your master — not because you became strong enough to defeat it, but because you changed jurisdictions. You moved from the domain of law into the domain of grace. Grace does not make sin acceptable; it makes righteousness possible. Under law, sin had dominion because failure was inevitable. Under grace, sin has lost its grip because Christ has already overcome it on your behalf. Your victory over sin is not something you are working toward — it is something you are working from.

James 4:7–8 gives the practical sequence: submit first, then resist. Many believers try to resist the devil in their own strength and wonder why they keep losing. The order matters. Submission to God — the very posture of holy fear we sat in yesterday — is what precedes effective resistance. Draw near to God. He will draw near to you. And in His nearness, lesser things lose their power.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I come to You today with honesty about my struggles. There are areas where I have fallen, areas where I keep falling, and areas where I am tempted to believe that victory is not possible for someone like me. I reject that lie today in the name of Jesus.

Sin is not my master. You have declared it, and I receive it. I am not under law — I am under grace. And today I choose to live from that reality rather than toward it. I submit myself fully to You — my will, my desires, my patterns, my weaknesses. I place them all under Your authority.

Where I am tempted today, remind me that You are faithful. Show me the way of escape. Let me be someone who runs toward You in the moment of temptation, not away from You in shame after it. Your nearness is my victory.

## Daily Practices

- Read Romans 6:1–14 in full. Let Paul's argument about being dead to sin and alive in Christ settle deeply before you move on with your day.
- Identify your most consistent area of temptation. Bring it specifically and honestly before God — no vague confessions today. Name it. Then receive the grace of 1 Corinthians 10:13 over it.
- Journal: "What does the 'way of escape' God provides look like practically in my most common temptation? What specific step could I take next time?"
- Find a trusted person in your life — an accountability partner or close friend in faith. Share one area you are believing God for breakthrough. Invite their prayer this week.
