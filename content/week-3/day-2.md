## Revelation

Three words. In the Greek, it is a single word: Tetelestai. It was stamped on commercial invoices in the ancient world — it meant "paid in full." When Jesus cried Tetelestai from the cross, He was not whispering a dying breath. He was making a legal declaration: the debt has been settled. The account is closed. Nothing more is owed. The cross is not an ongoing transaction — it is a completed one.

Colossians 2:14 makes this breathtakingly concrete. Every charge against you — every act of rebellion, every failure of love, every broken promise, every secret sin — was written on a record that stood against you and condemned you. And Christ took that record and nailed it to the cross. Not filed it away. Not reduced the sentence. Nailed it. To the same wood that held His body. The record that condemned you was destroyed at the same moment He was. It does not exist anymore.

And then the veil tears — from top to bottom, from heaven downward — and the Most Holy Place is now open. Always. To everyone. To you. The curtain that separated humanity from the presence of God is gone, and it was torn by God Himself. The way in is not your effort. The way in is His body. Come near. The door is permanently open.

## Morning Prayer

Lord Jesus, I hear You say it — Tetelestai. It is finished. And I receive it not as a historical footnote but as a present-tense reality that defines my life. The debt is paid. Every charge against me has been nailed to Your cross. I am not partly forgiven. I am not on probation. I am completely, irrevocably, fully reconciled.

Where shame has told me that my past still holds power over me, I declare: it was nailed to the cross. Where the enemy has whispered that I owe something more — I declare: it is finished. Nothing more is owed.

And I come near today through the open veil — not with timidity, not with the performance of someone trying to deserve access — but with the confidence of one who has been bought in, carried in, by the blood of Jesus. I am in Your presence. And I am welcome here.

## Amen

In Your finished name, Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read Colossians 2:13–15 in full. Picture the list of charges — every specific sin and failure — being physically nailed to wood. Watch it happen. Then let it go.
- Read Matthew 27:50–51 — the moment the veil tore. God tore it from the top down, not from the bottom up. Meditate on what that direction means.
- Journal: "What charge against me have I been carrying that Christ already nailed to the cross? Why have I been picking it back up?"
- Pray Hebrews 10:19–22 back to God as your declaration of confidence. Read it aloud as your own words. Enter boldly today.
