## Revelation

Ephesians 2:10 contains one of the most powerful words in the New Testament. The word translated "handiwork" is the Greek poiema — from which we get our word "poem." You are God's poem. His masterpiece. His creative work. Paul is saying: before he talks about what you are called to do, he is insisting on what you are called to be — a work of art, created in Christ Jesus.

The works were prepared in advance. Not vaguely. Not approximately. Specifically, intentionally, thoughtfully. God has already laid out the path; your task is not to invent your purpose but to discover and walk in what He has already prepared. And Romans 11:29 closes the door on every lie that says God has given up on you or revoked your calling because of what you have done or failed to do. His gifts and His call are irrevocable. He has not changed His mind about you. Not after your worst day. Not after your longest season of wandering.

Philippians 1:6 provides the deepest reassurance: He who began a good work in you will carry it to completion. Your purpose is not your responsibility to sustain — it is His commitment to complete. You are a poem He has not finished writing yet. The blank pages ahead of you are not evidence of your failure to find purpose. They are evidence that the Author is still working.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I receive this word today with both hands open. I am Your handiwork — Your poem — and You do not abandon unfinished work. What You have started in me, You will complete.

I release the anxiety of having to figure out my purpose on my own. You prepared the works in advance. My calling is not a mystery I must solve — it is a path You have already laid. Give me the eyes to see it, the courage to walk it, and the patience to trust You when the next step is not yet visible.

And where I have disqualified myself — told myself that I have forfeited my calling through failure or inconsistency — I receive Your Word: the call is irrevocable. You have not changed Your mind about me.

## Daily Practices

- Read Ephesians 2:1–10 in full. Notice how Paul moves from what we were — dead in transgressions — to what we now are — God's handiwork. The contrast is the gospel. Let it land.
- Journal: "What gifts, passions, or abilities has God placed in me that feel connected to a larger purpose? What has stopped me from fully stepping into them?"
- Write down one "good work" — however small — that you sense God is specifically inviting you into this season. Name it as concretely as you can. Then ask: what is my next step?
- Pray Philippians 1:6 back to God not as a request but as a declaration of trust: "I am confident that You who began a good work in me will carry it to completion." Say it aloud. Mean it.
