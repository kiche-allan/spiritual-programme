## Revelation

Habakkuk 3:17–18 is perhaps the most audacious act of trust in all of Scripture. The prophet is not describing a season of abundance and then praising God for it. He is standing in the middle of total collapse — no figs, no grapes, no olives, no sheep, no cattle — and choosing joy. This is not denial. Habakkuk sees the emptiness clearly. He lists it precisely, item by item. And then he says: yet. That single word carries the full weight of a faith that has learned to rest in the character of God when the circumstances offer nothing to stand on.

Proverbs 3:5–6 gives us the anatomy of this kind of trust. With all your heart — not partial trust, not cautious optimism, not "I'll trust God but keep my own backup plan." All. And lean not on your own understanding — your analysis of the situation, however accurate, is partial. God's vision includes dimensions you cannot see from where you are standing. Trust does not require you to stop thinking. It requires you to hold your conclusions loosely, in submission to a God whose wisdom is higher than yours.

Romans 8:28 carries an active verb that is easy to miss: God works. He is not passively allowing your suffering. He is actively, sovereignly, continuously weaving every circumstance — including the painful ones, the confusing ones, the ones that seem like waste — into something redemptive. You cannot always see the weaving from the inside of the tapestry. But He sees it from above. And He has not stopped working.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I bring You today the circumstances that make trust most difficult — the unanswered prayers, the unresolved situations, the things that still look like empty fields and bare vines. I do not pretend they are not there. I see them clearly. And yet.

Yet I will rejoice in the Lord. Not because the circumstances have changed — they have not. But because You have not changed. You are still faithful. You are still working. You are still weaving things I cannot see into something I will one day marvel at. My trust is not in my ability to understand what You are doing. My trust is in the character of the One who is doing it.

I lean not on my own understanding today. I hold my plans, my analysis, my timelines, and my expectations loosely — and I submit them to You. Make my paths straight, Lord.

## Daily Practices

- Read Habakkuk 3:1–19 in full — the full arc of the prophet's journey from complaint to confident trust. Let his progression become yours. Note what changed in him before anything changed in his circumstances.
- Write down your "empty fields" — the specific circumstances where trust is hardest right now. Then write Romans 8:28 beneath each one. God is actively working in these too.
- Practise the "yet I will rejoice" discipline today. Before any circumstances change, choose one act of deliberate worship — a song, a prayer of gratitude, a declaration. Not for the circumstances, but for who God is in them.
- Read Genesis 50:20 — Joseph's summary of his life: "You intended to harm me, but God intended it for good." Think of one past situation where you can now see God's weaving. Let it build your faith for where you are now.
