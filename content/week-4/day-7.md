## Revelation

The week closes not in exhaustion but in rest — the particular, hard-won rest of someone who has confessed honestly, repented genuinely, chosen holiness daily, armed themselves for battle, and learned to trust in the dark. This is not the rest of someone who has avoided the fight. It is the rest of someone who has been in it and discovered that the One fighting alongside them cannot lose.

Revelation 12:11 reveals the weapons of the overcomer: the blood of the Lamb and the word of their testimony. You overcome not by being stronger than the enemy but by being covered by what Christ did. His blood — the finished work of the cross — is your legal basis for every victory. And your testimony — what God has done in your life — is a weapon, because it declares in real and personal terms that God is faithful. Your story of God's faithfulness is not just comfort for yourself. It is a weapon against the darkness.

John 16:33 contains perhaps the most honest and yet most reassuring promise in the Gospels. Jesus does not promise you a life without trouble. He says plainly: in this world you will have trouble. But then — But take heart! I have overcome the world. He has already overcome the very world that is troubling you. The battles are real. The challenges are real. But the verdict is already in. You are more than a conqueror — not because you are fighting hard enough, but because you are in Christ, and in Christ the victory is settled, permanent, and yours.

## Morning Prayer

Father, I close this week in a posture of rest — not because everything is resolved, but because I know the One in whom all things are resolved. I have confessed what needed to be brought into the light. I have turned from what needed to be left behind. I have chosen holiness not as a burden but as a way of life. I have put on Your armour. I have trusted You in the dark.

And now I rest. Not the rest of the passive or the defeated, but the rest of the overcomer — the one who has fought from a position of victory and returned to that place of peace. The blood of the Lamb covers me. My testimony speaks of Your faithfulness. The world that troubles me has already been overcome by the One who lives in me.

Send me into the coming week as a person who knows they cannot be ultimately defeated. Not because my circumstances are favourable, but because You have overcome.

## Daily Practices

- Review the whole week — which theme landed most deeply? Confession, repentance, holiness, warfare, or trust? Write one sentence that captures what God said to you through that theme. Carry it forward.
- Share one insight from this week with someone in your life — a friend, a partner, a member of your faith community. Your testimony is a weapon. Use it deliberately.
- Identify one concrete change — in habit, in practice, in posture before God — that you are committing to carry forward from this week into the next. Be specific, not vague.
- Read Romans 8:31–39 as your closing declaration. Let Paul's unanswerable questions be your war cry: If God is for us, who can be against us?
