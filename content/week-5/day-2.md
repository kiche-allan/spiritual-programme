## Revelation

The mourning Jesus speaks of in the second Beatitude is not grief over personal loss or difficult circumstances — it is mourning over sin. It is the response of a soul that has seen something of the holiness of God and something of its own condition in that light. This mourning is not despair — it is the grief of someone who genuinely cares. And it is precisely this mourning that the blood of Jesus was shed to address. When the conscience is pierced, when mourning arises over what we have done and who we have been, the blood of Christ is God's answer — not scolding, not distance, but comfort. They will be comforted.

Hebrews 9:14 makes an astonishing claim about the blood. It does not merely forgive — it cleanses the conscience. The guilt that sits in the deepest part of you — the memories that surface at night, the shame that has shaped how you see yourself — the blood of Christ has the power to reach that deep and clean it. Not cover it. Not manage it. Cleanse it. From acts that lead to death, so that we may serve the living God. The purpose of the cleansing is not just inner peace — it is restored capacity for purpose.

The fourth Beatitude — hunger and thirst for righteousness — speaks to transformed appetite. This is the natural fruit of having been cleansed by the blood: you begin to want what God wants. The person who has truly wept over their sin and received the comfort of forgiveness does not return to that sin easily. Their appetite has changed. They hunger now for what is right, what is true, what is holy — and the promise is that this hunger will be satisfied. They will be filled. God does not leave holy hunger unmet.

## Morning Prayer

Lord Jesus, I come to You today with the grief that genuine faith produces — mourning over the ways I have fallen short, the sin that has separated me from the fullness of what You intended for me. I do not come in despair. I come in the confidence that this mourning is the very thing that draws Your comfort.

I apply the blood of Jesus over my conscience today. Not as a formula — as a faith-filled act of receiving what was purchased for me at immeasurable cost. Every charge that my own heart brings against me, every memory that tries to define me by my worst moments — I bring it under the covering of the blood. Cleanse me. Not just forgive — cleanse. So that I may serve You with a clear conscience and a free heart.

And awaken in me a genuine hunger and thirst for righteousness. Not performance. Not religion. Actual righteousness — the character of Christ, pressed into the fabric of my daily life. Let me want what You want. Let the appetites the world has cultivated in me be replaced by this one, clean, holy hunger. Fill me, Lord. I am empty and I am asking.

## Daily Practices

- Read Hebrews 9:11–14 in full — the full argument for why the blood of Christ is categorically different from any other offering. Let the weight of "how much more" land on you personally.
- Spend time in honest mourning before God — not manufactured emotion, but genuine acknowledgment of specific ways you have fallen short this past week. Name them. Then receive the comfort of 1 John 1:7 over each one.
- Journal: "What do I currently hunger and thirst for? Does the answer reflect a transformed appetite — or the same appetites the world has shaped in me?" Ask God to realign your desires with His.
- Read Psalm 51 — David's great prayer after sin, which moves from mourning to petition to purpose. Pray it as your own prayer today. Let verse 10 be your declaration: "Create in me a pure heart, O God."
