## Revelation

We return this Easter Monday not to a theological lecture but to the cross itself — slowly, without rushing past it. Because what happened at Golgotha was not a stepping stone — it was the hinge of all of history. Isaiah 53 was written seven hundred years before the crucifixion, and yet it reads like an eyewitness account. Pierced. Crushed. Punished. Wounded. Every word is deliberate, every word is personal — and every word is for you.

Romans 5:8 contains the single most staggering sentence in all of theology. Not that Christ died for the righteous. Not that He died for those who had already cleaned up their lives. While we were still sinners — in the full ugliness of our rebellion — He died for us. The cross was not God's response to your improvement. It was God's response to your need. You did not have to become worthy of it. It was given to you while you were at your worst.

And Matthew 27:46 brings us to the most agonising moment of all — the cry of dereliction. Jesus, quoting Psalm 22, cried out in the darkness: My God, my God, why have you forsaken me? Here is the revelation that should stop you completely: Christ experienced the full weight of separation from the Father so that you would never have to. The distance you feared between you and God — He entered it. He exhausted it. He closed it. Forever.

## Morning Prayer

Lord Jesus, I return this week to the foot of the cross. Not hurrying past it. Not leaving it behind. I want to sit again with the full weight of what You did — not as theology alone, but as love directed at me personally.

You were pierced for my transgressions. Not for transgressions in the abstract — for mine, specifically, by name, known before time. The punishment that brought me peace was placed on You. I receive that today with tears of gratitude I cannot fully express.

And in Your cry of dereliction, I find my deepest comfort: You have been where I was. You entered every darkness I have ever known. The forsaking You experienced means I will never be forsaken. Thank You. I do not have the words — but You know my heart.

## Amen

In Your precious name, Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read Isaiah 53 in full, slowly. Read it as a letter written to you personally. Every pronoun — he was pierced for our transgressions, by his wounds we are healed — make it specific. This is about you.
- Read the crucifixion account in John 19. Sit with the details — the thorns, the nails, the darkness, the thirst, the moment He gave up His spirit. Do not rush past them.
- Journal: "What specific sin or shame of mine did Christ carry on the cross?" Name it. Then leave it there. Do not pick it back up.
- Spend 10 minutes in silence before the cross — not asking for anything. Only receiving what has already been given. Let the silence be gratitude.
