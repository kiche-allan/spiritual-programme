## Revelation

The fear of God is one of the most misunderstood realities in the Christian life. For many, it conjures images of a distant, severe deity watching for mistakes — a fear that shrinks you. But the biblical fear of God is not that. It is not the fear of a slave before a cruel master. It is the awe of a child who suddenly glimpses the full height and depth of their Father's greatness.

Notice that Proverbs 9:10 declares it the beginning of wisdom — not wisdom itself, but its starting point. Everything else flows from here. Your ability to make good decisions, to walk in purpose, to hear God's voice, to live with integrity — all of it is downstream of whether you have a right, reverent, wonder-filled understanding of who God is. A small view of God produces a small life. An accurate view of God — holy, infinite, near, loving — produces everything.

Proverbs 19:23 reveals what holy fear actually produces in a life: contentment, rest, and a life untouched by the kind of trouble that comes from living outside God's design. And Isaiah 66:2 shows us the posture God Himself is looking for across the whole earth — he who trembles at my word. Not trembling with terror, but trembling with reverence. When you open Scripture, does something in you shift? Does the weight of it land? That trembling is not weakness — it is precisely what catches God's eye. Begin this week here, in wonder, and let everything else flow from that place.

## Morning Prayer

Holy Father, I begin this week not with a to-do list, but with a posture — face down in wonder before You. I confess that I have sometimes made You too small in my thinking. I have approached You casually when You are glorious. I have treated Your grace as ordinary when it is extraordinary beyond measure.

Teach me to fear You rightly. Not with a fear that paralyses, but with an awe that transforms. Let me see You today — not partially, not through the fog of my assumptions — but clearly, as You truly are: infinite in holiness, overflowing in mercy, closer than my next breath.

Displace every lesser fear in me — fear of failure, of people's opinions, of the unknown — and replace them all with this one clean, life-giving awe of who You are. Let the fear of You be the ground I stand on all week. Let it be the beginning of everything.

## Amen

In Jesus' name, Amen.

## Daily Practices

- Read Isaiah 6:1–8 — Isaiah's encounter with holy fear and what it produced in him immediately. Notice what he saw, what he said, and what God commissioned him to do as a result.
- Journal: "In what areas of my life have I made God too small or too familiar? What would change if I carried a constant awareness of His holiness?"
- Sit in silence for 10 minutes with no words and no agenda. Simply arrive before God as He is — not as you need Him to be. Let Him be large in the quiet.
- Write three attributes of God that feel most beyond your comprehension today. Choose one and meditate on it slowly for five minutes. Let wonder do its work.
